Improved Euler Method

The Improved Euler Method, also called Heun’s Method, is used to approximate the solution of an initial value problem:


$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = f(x,y) $$ $$ y(x_0)=y_0 $$

Ordinary Euler’s Method uses only the slope at the beginning of the interval. Heun’s Method improves accuracy by averaging the starting slope and predicted ending slope.

Algorithm

Predictor

$$ y_p = y_n + h f(x_n,y_n) $$
Corrector

$$ y_{n+1} = y_n + \frac{h}{2} [f(x_n,y_n)+f(x_{n+1},y_p)] $$
Why Use Heun Method?

Better Accuracy

Heun’s Method improves the ordinary Euler method by averaging two slopes.

Easy to Implement

It is simple to code and useful for solving first-order differential equations numerically.

Applications

Used in engineering, physics, population models, and numerical simulations.

Formula Summary

Predictor → estimate next value

Corrector → improve approximation
